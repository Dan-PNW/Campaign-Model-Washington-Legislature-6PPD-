House 2026 track record & 2027 strategic read

What happened: HB 2421 (Hall, D-5) was heard in House ENVI on Jan 13, 2026 (Toxic-Free Future, Trout Unlimited, AWC, NWIFC testified for; USTMA, Les Schwab, WA Trucking testified against). The committee moved a 1st substitute, then Appropriations moved a 2nd substitute. The bill died in Rules/calendar at sine die — never reached the House floor.

The difference from the Senate: the Senate bill (Liias) never even got an exec session. The House bill cleared two committees. In vote math, the House is safe — the problem is floor time and leadership prioritization, not member counts.

2027 target: Rep. Hall returns as prime sponsor. Co-sponsors Berry and Ramel are both on ENVI. If the Senate passes early in session (February or earlier), there is floor time in the House. Speaker Jinkins' calendar decision is the critical ask. The EPC slate is the lever — a priority bill with 26-org backing gets scheduled; an orphan bill gets sine die'd again.

Approps chair watch: Ormsby's retirement creates an unknown for 2027. The substitute path (fee stripped or redesigned) worked in 2026 — plan for the same flexibility. Build a relationship with whoever chairs Approps in 2027-28.
