9
Concurrence or conference committee context
If the second chamber amends the bill — most likely on fee design — the first chamber must either concur or the two chambers convene a conference committee to reconcile differences. Not independently modeled, but worth planning for: a clean concurrence costs a day; a conference committee can cost a week you may not have late in session.
10
Governor's signature context
Once both chambers pass identical language, the bill goes to the Governor, who can sign, let it become law without signature, or veto (overridable by two-thirds of both chambers). Not modeled numerically — a bill that clears both chambers on an environmental-health priority is a low-risk veto target in Washington — but it's a real step, not a formality to skip in planning.

Passage isn't the finish line. Distribution of simulated floor outcomes (20,000 roll calls)
Vote correlation ρ: 0.35
Senators don't vote independently — budget news, caucus mood, and leadership pressure move them together. Each trial now draws a shared session shock (Gaussian copula). ρ = 0 reproduces the old independent model, which was overconfident: too-narrow a distribution. ρ ≈ 0.3–0.5 is realistic for a same-caucus bloc. Higher ρ fattens both tails — more landslide passes and more collapses.

Method & data honesty

What is real: the Senate and House committee rosters, sponsors, the 2026 procedural history (SB 6119: hearing only, no exec session; HB 2421: cleared ENVI + Approps, died at calendar), hearing testimony and testifiers, retirements, the litigation, the budget, Flexsys, and every named organization.

What is estimated (by design, awaiting your input): base leans (heuristics from party, sponsorship, committee, district — labeled per senator with confidence level), relationship depths (editable on Senate → Relationships), opposition mobilization, and the engine constants. Donor ties are EMPTY until you pull PDC Contributions to Candidates.

Confidence levels: high (±5 pts) = clear on-record signals. Medium (±12 pts) = party + district + committee. Low (±18 pts) = limited public signal; treat the range seriously, not just the midpoint. The ranges are honest uncertainty bands, not statistical confidence intervals.

Engine: Monte Carlo (20,000 trials) over per-senator vote probabilities with correlated voting — a one-factor Gaussian copula draws a shared session shock per trial (budget news, caucus mood, leadership pressure) so senators move together; correlation ρ is adjustable on the whip tab (default 0.35; ρ = 0 reproduces independent voting). Marginal per-senator probabilities are preserved exactly. Stage-gate pathway multiplies six sequential gates; BDM weighted-median bargaining model produces the landing point. Relationship depths are mutable state — edit them without touching code.

Sensitivity (tornado): the Forecast tab re-runs the engine one input at a time — each factor to its worst/best state, each gate ±15 pts, EPC toggled, opposition ±25%, ρ perturbed — and ranks inputs by their swing in P(law). One-at-a-time by construction: real inputs correlate, so read it as a ranking of where to spend effort, not a prediction interval.
